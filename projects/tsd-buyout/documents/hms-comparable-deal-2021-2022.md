# Comparable Deal — Hallmark Management Service (HMS) 50% Buyout

> 🔒 **Confidential — peer's deal.** Provided by a business-owner friend of Rick's who used Plante Moran for his own partner buyout. Held here strictly as a **comparable / precedent** for the TSD deal, not a Revolution Group transaction. Don't share externally.

**Source PDFs (this folder):**
- `HM Valuation - HMS FINAL 2021 FMV Valuation Report.pdf` (Plante & Moran, 81 pp.)
- `HM Stock Redemption Agreement - signed 3-4-22.pdf` (signed 3-4-2022, 3 pp.)

Related: [Plante Moran engagement letter note](plante-moran-hallmark-valuation-engagement-2021.md) — same deal, the engagement that produced the valuation below.

## The deal in one line

A 50% co-owner (Thomas Walters) was bought out of HMS by the company; independent appraisal pegged the stake at **$1,431,800**, but it **closed at $1,233,300** — structured **~35% cash / 65% seller note**.

## Valuation (Plante & Moran, as of 6/30/2021)

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Interest valued | 50.0% equity, **non-marketable, non-controlling** |
| Standard of value | Fair market value (IRS Rev. Rul. 59-60); USPAP / ASA / AICPA SSVS |
| Primary method | **Income approach — discounted cash flow (DCF)** |
| Long-term growth | 3.0% |
| Capitalization rate | 17.0% (rounded; WACC less growth) |
| Discount for limited control | **5.0%** |
| Discount for lack of marketability (DLOM) | **5.0%** |
| **Concluded FMV of 50% interest** | **$1,431,800** |

Implied ~$3.17M for 100% before the two 5% discounts.

## Redemption terms (signed 3-4-2022)

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Structure | Company **redeems** the departing 50% holder's 400 shares (redemption, not a cross-purchase) |
| **Purchase price** | **$1,233,300** — *below* the $1,431,800 appraisal (~86%) |
| Payment | **$433,300 cash at closing** + **$800,000 promissory note** (seller financing) |
| Non-compete | 5 years |
| Non-solicitation | 5 years (employees + customers) |
| Departing owner | Resigns as employee, officer, director; mutual release |
| Note security | Company keeps life-insurance premiums current on both principals until the note is paid |
| Misc | Departing owner gets 50% of net collections on a named A/R (Ohio Valley University, ~$97K) |
| Governing law | Ohio |

## Why this matters for TSD

- **Price came in below the independent valuation.** Confirms the CPA advisor's "leeway in price vs. terms" — an appraisal is an anchor, not a ceiling.
- **Seller financing was the norm here, not all-cash.** ~65% of this deal was a seller note. Polly wants all cash; this is a concrete precedent for offering a higher headline price in exchange for a note — see [../financials.md](../financials.md).
- **The discounts are modest (5% + 5%).** For a 50/50 deadlock-prone ownership, Plante Moran applied only 5% for limited control and 5% DLOM. Useful expectation-setting if Carlos argues for larger or smaller discounts.
- **DCF was the driver**, not an EBITDA multiple — relevant given TSD's near-break-even EBITDA makes a multiple approach awkward.
- **Standard protective terms**: 5-yr non-compete/non-solicit, mutual release, life insurance securing the note. A template for what Ice Miller should paper for Rick.
