## Why Cindy facilitates

The facilitator runs the *process* so the owner (Rick) can be a *participant*. Your job is time, flow, and equal airtime — not having the answers. When Rick is talking as a participant, you're the one keeping the room on track. That separation is the whole point.

## The core framing — TSD vs. RG

This is a **TSD business-unit** session. The single most useful thing you'll do is keep the room on *what TSD owns and can move*. When a discussion drifts to a company-wide Revolution Group item (corporate policy, RG-level strategy, anything "that's a company thing"), name it and move it to the RG section of [rocks.md](rocks.md) as a dependency — not a TSD Rock. Say: **"Is that ours to own, or is that an RG company item? If it's RG, it goes on the dependency list, not our Rocks."**

## Your 5 jobs

1. **Keep time.** Call each section's clock out loud. Protect the Rocks block — it's the point of the day.
2. **Draw everyone out.** Every attendee speaks in every section. If someone's quiet, ask them directly.
3. **Park rabbit holes.** When a tangent starts, name it, drop it on the Issues List, move on. "Good one — parking it, issue list."
4. **Run IDS on issues.** Identify the real issue → Discuss once → Solve (decide an action + owner). Don't let discussion loop.
5. **Force the close.** Every Rock and action item gets a single named owner and a date before anyone leaves.

## Agenda (4 hours)

| Time | Block | Minutes | What happens |
|------|-------|---------|--------------|
| 1:00 | **Check-in** | 15 | Each person: one personal win + one business win since last quarter. Sets tone, gets everyone talking early. |

| 2:15 | **Break** | 10 | |
| 2:25 | **Build the issues list** | 30 | Everyone dumps issues/obstacles/ideas onto one list. No solving yet — just capture. |
| 2:55 | **Set Q3 Rocks** | 75 | The main event. Propose → debate → cut to 3–7 company Rocks. Assign one owner + a "done =" to each. Then each person sets their own 1–3 individual Rocks. |

> Running long? Protect **Set Q3 Rocks** and **Close**. Cut IDS down — unsolved issues carry to the weekly meeting.

## Facilitator phrases that work

- **To move on:** "Let's park that — onto the issue list. Moving on."
- **To draw out:** "Ryan, we haven't heard from you on this — what do you see?"
- **To cut a Rock debate:** "Is this a top-7 priority for the next 90 days, yes or no?"
- **To force the close:** "Who owns it, and by when? Saying it out loud."
- **When Rick dominates:** "Rick, hold that — I want to hear the room first."

## Ground rules (read these out at the start)

- Phones down. In-person for a reason.
- One conversation at a time.
- Disagree in the room, commit when you leave.
- A Rock is **done or not done** — no "80% there."
- Fewer Rocks > more Rocks. If everything's a priority, nothing is.
