# RFP Process Policy

**Owner:** Revolution Group  
**Effective:** 2026-05-15  
**Review:** Annually

---

## Purpose

Ensure all significant vendor and partner purchases follow a consistent evaluation process, protect the company from unfavorable terms, and document decisions for audit and accountability purposes.

---

## Scope

Applies to any procurement where:
- Annual value exceeds **$10,000**, OR
- The contract term exceeds **12 months**, OR
- The vendor will have access to company or client data

---

## Process

### 1. Define Requirements
- Document the business need, technical requirements, and budget before soliciting vendors.
- Owner: department head or project sponsor.

### 2. Identify Vendors
- Minimum **3 vendors** solicited unless a sole-source justification is documented.
- Sole-source exceptions require VP or CEO approval.

### 3. Issue the RFP
- RFP must include: scope of work, evaluation criteria, response deadline, and contact.
- All vendors receive the same document and have equal access to Q&A responses.

### 4. Evaluate Responses
- Responses scored against pre-defined criteria (cost, capability, references, security posture, contract terms).
- Evaluation team: minimum 2 people including the budget owner.

### 5. Select and Document
- Document the selected vendor, score summary, and rationale.
- Rejected vendors noted with brief reason.
- Log decision in `decisions/log.md` for significant procurements.

### 6. Contract Review
- Contracts over $25,000 or with unusual terms reviewed by legal or an officer before signing.
- NDA executed before sharing any confidential information with finalists.

### 7. Vendor Onboarding
- Selected vendor added to vendor list with contact, contract dates, and renewal date.
- If vendor accesses client data: confirm NDA is executed and added to vendor NDA list (SOC audit control 6.19).

---

## Exceptions

Exceptions require written approval from the CEO or VP and must be documented.
