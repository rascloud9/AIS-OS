# Security Policy

**Owner:** Revolution Group  
**Effective:** 2026-05-15  
**Review:** Annually

---

We are required to remind you annually of the importance of maintaining the security and confidentiality of our company's applications and data as well as the information that belongs to our customers and vendors. The viability of our business and all businesses depends on the protection of information.

---

- **Confidentiality** — All information accessed, whether actual data or knowledge of process and procedure, is confidential and must be protected. This includes our company, customer, and vendor data as well as knowledge about business processes and procedures.

- **Single Sign On (SSO)** — All company applications and data must be accessed via Single Sign On (SSO) where available. This means that you will only need to log in once to access all our SSO-supported applications.

- **Multi-factor authentication** — In addition to SSO, we require the use of multi-factor authentication. This means that you will need to provide additional verification, such as a code sent to your phone, to access our applications.

- **Unique passwords** — All passwords must be unique for every application and stored in a password manager such as LastPass. This will help prevent unauthorized access to our applications. All passwords must comply with the current complexity policy.

- **Personal devices** — If company data is accessed with a personal device (such as a mobile phone) the company may remotely erase the company data or all the device data, if necessary, in the event of a security problem such as a loss of the device.

- **Anti-virus and anti-malware software** — Any computer used to access company or customer information systems must be configured with anti-virus and anti-malware software that is kept up to date. This will help protect against viruses and other malicious software.

- **Loss or theft of equipment** — If you lose control of any object (laptop, phone, briefcase, documents, etc.) that potentially has company or customer data you must contact your manager immediately who will inform the Security Team.

- **Ransomware** — If you receive a suspicious email attachment, link, or pop-up demanding payment, do not click, open, or pay. Disconnect from the network immediately and contact your manager or the Security Team. Time matters — a fast report limits damage.

- **Annual Security Training** — You must attend security training at least once per calendar year. This training is offered quarterly for your convenience.

- **Monthly Security Training** — You must complete the security training sessions that are assigned to you each month.

- **Annual Security Policy Acknowledgement** — You must read and sign the current security policy (this document) annually at the beginning of the calendar year.

- **Disciplinary Action** — Failure to adhere to the Security Policy may result in disciplinary action, including possible termination.

---

**What to do if you have questions or suspect a security or confidentiality breach:** If you suspect or detect either a security breach or a confidentiality breach, please contact your manager or the Revolution Group Director of Technology Services immediately.
