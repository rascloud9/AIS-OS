# Interview Questions — Michael Sweet

## 1. The BPS Departure (answer this first)

- Walk me through your time at BPS. You were there one year — what happened?
- When you say the position was eliminated, was that your role specifically or a broader reduction?
- What did you learn about selling managed IT services in that year that you didn't know going in?

---

## 2. Hunter Instincts

- Take me through your prospecting process — how do you build a pipeline from scratch in a new territory?
- At Fireproof you signed 400+ new customers in 11 years. What was your lead generation mix — cold outreach, referrals, events, chamber relationships?
- When a deal stalls, what's your move?
- What's the longest sales cycle you've navigated? Walk me through it.

---

## 3. MSP / Managed Security Knowledge

- How would you describe Revolution Group's value proposition to a CFO who thinks IT is just a cost center?
- What do you know about the difference between managed IT support and managed security? How would you sell the security angle?
- Most of our clients are SMBs — 25 to 250 employees. How does your approach change for that buyer vs. the enterprise or channel deals you've done?
- Cybersecurity is a growing part of our story. How comfortable are you selling something technically complex that you're still learning?

---

## 4. Network and Market

- Who do you know in Columbus that could turn into a deal in the first 90 days?
- You've worked the Columbus Chamber and the Bar Association. What active relationships do you have right now that are warm?
- You mentioned you know Randa Gallagher — how do you know her and how recently have you been in touch?

---

## 5. Comp and Expectations

- Your current total comp is around $120K. What does the right deal look like for you?
- How much of that do you need to be base vs. at-risk?
- What does your first-year pipeline need to look like for you to feel like you made the right move?
- How long are you willing to invest before expecting commissions to offset a lower base?

---

## 6. Fit and Motivation

- You met with me about 10 years ago. What's different about what you're looking for now vs. then?
- Revolution Group has been around since 1995 — we're not a startup, but we're also not a big corporate. What's your ideal company size and culture?
- What would make you leave this role in two years?
- What do you need from a sales leader to do your best work?

---

## Red Flags to Listen For
- Vague answer on BPS — "it just didn't work out" without specifics
- Overconfidence on MSP knowledge he doesn't actually have
- Heavy reliance on inbound or marketing-generated leads
- Expects a high base with minimal at-risk — wrong profile for a hunter role
- Can't name specific Columbus prospects or warm contacts
