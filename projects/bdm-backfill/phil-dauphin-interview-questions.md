# Interview Questions — Phil Dauphin (Cindy)

**Context for Cindy:** Rick already covered Phil's background, deal history, managed services experience, and why he's leaving Strategic Systems. These questions are designed to clarify a few things that were fuzzy in Rick's interview and to get a read on fit, communication style, and how Phil would represent the brand.

---

## 1. Clarify the Managed Services vs. Staffing Question

Rick probed this but didn't get a crisp answer. Phil described deals at Star Seven Six / Careworks as $45K–$60K/month managed services — but it's not clear whether that was true fully-managed IT (like what Revolution Group does) or managed staffing/staff augmentation. Try a different angle:

- When you were selling those managed services deals at Star Seven Six, what was the actual deliverable? Were Revolution Group's engineers on-site at the client, or were they embedded at the client as dedicated headcount?
- How did you handle the renewal conversation — did the client feel like they were buying an outcome, or buying hours?
- Revolution Group's model is fully managed: the client calls us when something breaks, we handle everything, fixed monthly fee. Have you sold that model specifically, or was there always an element of "we'll provide resources and you direct them"?

---

## 2. The CGI Gap (short tenure — 2023 to 2024)

The resume shows CGI as VP Sales for roughly one year. Rick didn't dig into this.

- Tell me about CGI — VP Sales sounds like a big step. What drew you there and what happened?
- Were you building a team, inheriting one, or both?
- What was the outcome — did you leave, or were you let go?

---

## 3. How He'd Represent Revolution Group Externally

Cindy is marketing — this is her lane.

- If you ran into a prospect at a Columbus Chamber event tomorrow and they asked what Revolution Group does, how would you describe us in 30 seconds?
- We've invested a lot in our brand and reputation. When you're out in the market, how do you make sure you're representing a company's brand consistently vs. going off-script?
- Have you ever been in a situation where you said something to a prospect that didn't align with what your company could actually deliver? How did you handle that?

---

## 4. Listening vs. Talking

Phil mentioned his "90/10 rule" — he listens 90% and talks 10%. Probe whether that's real or just a thing he says.

- Give me an example of a time when really listening to a prospect changed what you proposed or how you approached the deal.
- What's the most common mistake salespeople make in the first meeting with a new prospect?
- What do you do when a prospect dominates the conversation and you can't get to your discovery questions?

---

## 5. Culture and Longevity

Phil has had a lot of stops — Smart IT, vtrIT, Workstate, Star Seven Six (which became Sedgwick, then Careworks), CGI, Strategic Systems. That's 6+ companies in 15 years. Some were acquisitions, some were his choice. Get a feel for what he's actually looking for.

- You've been at a lot of companies. What's the common thread in the ones where you did your best work?
- What specifically about Revolution Group makes you think this is different from the other opportunities you could be talking to right now?
- If you joined us and it was going well — what does year three look like for you here?
- What's the thing that would make you pick up the phone and take a recruiter's call two years from now?

---

## 6. Working with Marketing

- Have you ever worked closely with a marketing function — co-op campaigns, event support, content, outreach? What worked well?
- If you wanted to generate more awareness in a specific vertical or neighborhood of Columbus, what would you ask marketing to help you with?
- What's the most effective marketing support you've received from a company — and what was the least effective?

---

## Red Flags to Listen For

- Can't cleanly describe the difference between staffing and managed IT — suggests his MSP experience is shallower than represented
- Vague or contradictory answer on CGI — short VP tenure with no clear reason is worth digging on
- Brand "freelancer" mentality — describes pitching clients his way regardless of company messaging
- Talks more than listens in the interview itself — ironic given his stated philosophy
- "I just want stability" as the main motivation — right answer, wrong reason if that's the whole story
- Can't articulate why Revolution Group specifically, just that it "sounds like a great fit"
