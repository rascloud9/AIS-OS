# Interview Guide — Indica Martin

## What We Know Going In

**Strengths to validate:** Genuine SMB direct-sales motion, high-volume outbound habits, consistent top performer.  
**Risks to probe:** No IT/MSP product knowledge, 3-month Ahola tenure, Thryv is high-volume commodity selling (not consultative long-cycle).

- Test MSP model comprehension before she gets too far — don't explain it, see if she can figure it out.
- Surface comp in the first 10 minutes. Don't soft-pedal it.
- Push on deal size and actual closes, not just activity metrics.
- Probe the Ahola tenure directly and early.

## Recommended Format

**Step 1 — Phone screen (20–25 min)**  
Goal: Ahola explanation, comp check, energy/communication style. Go/no-go before investing a full hour.

**Step 2 — Full interview with Rick (45–60 min) if phone screen passes**  
**Step 3 — Vince follow-up if Rick's interview is positive**

---

## Phone Screen Questions (20 min)

1. **"Walk me through Ahola — you were there three months. What happened?"**  
   *(Listen for: candid explanation vs. vague deflection. Acceptable: role wasn't what was advertised, culture mismatch, better opportunity. Red flag: evasive or blames the company without specifics.)*

2. **"At Thryv you're doing 200 outbound calls a week. What's a typical deal size, and how long does it take to close?"**  
   *(Establish baseline. If ACV is $2-5K and close is 1-2 calls, that's commodity selling — different from a $60K/year MSP contract. Doesn't disqualify her, but need to know if she can shift gears.)*

3. **"What are you looking for in your next move — and what does the right comp structure look like?"**  
   *(Get base + total comp expectations early. If she's at $55-65K base now and needs $85K+, have that conversation on the phone, not after a 60-minute interview.)*

4. **"What do you know about Revolution Group and what we do?"**  
   *(Did she research? Tells you how serious she is. Doesn't need to be perfect — but zero research is a yellow flag.)*

## Full Interview Questions

### 1. The Ahola Question (if not resolved on phone screen)
- You were at Ahola for three months before leaving. Tell me more about that.
- Was that your call or theirs?

### 2. Hunter Motion — Volume vs. Consultative

The risk here is that Thryv is a churn-and-burn outbound model. Need to know if she can run a longer, more consultative sale.

- At Thryv, walk me through a deal from first call to signed contract. How many touches? How long?
- What's the most complex sale you've navigated — longest cycle, most stakeholders, biggest ACV?
- When you're 90 days into a new territory with no pipeline, what do you do in the first two weeks?
- At Dex Media you generated 70–100 new accounts per year for over a decade. Were those mostly cold starts or did you have inbound leads to work from?

### 3. The SMB Buyer — Can She Make the Leap?

Don't explain Revolution Group's model. See if she can get there herself.

- Our clients are small and mid-size businesses — 25 to 200 employees. Most of them have no internal IT. What problem do you think they're trying to solve when they hire a company like us?  
  *(Listen for: business continuity, security, cost certainty, time/distraction. If she jumps straight to "you sell tech stuff" without getting to the business pain, probe further.)*
- You've sold CRM and marketing software to business owners. How is selling IT services similar to that conversation — and how is it different?
- How technical do you try to get in a sales conversation, and when do you bring in technical help?  
  *(Critical question. Phil's failure mode was always needing a SE. We need someone who can develop enough product credibility to hold the room, even if not deeply technical.)*

### 4. Pipeline Discipline

- Walk me through how you manage your pipeline. What CRM are you using and how do you structure your week?
- What's your current pipeline value at Thryv — how much active opportunity do you have open right now?
- What's your close rate on qualified opportunities?
- Tell me about a deal you lost that you thought you had. What happened?

### 5. Columbus Network

- Who do you know in Columbus right now that could be a warm conversation in the first 60 days here?
- You've been selling to SMB owners in Columbus for 20+ years. What industries are you strongest in?
- How do you stay in touch with past clients and prospects — what's your system?

### 6. Comp and Expectations

*(If not resolved on phone screen)*

- What's your current total comp — base plus commissions?
- What does the right deal look like for you at a new company?
- How much of your comp do you want guaranteed vs. at-risk? Why?
- How long are you willing to work a pipeline before expecting commissions to make up the difference?

### 7. Fit and Motivation

- You've been in the same industry (Dex → YP → Thryv) for most of your career. What's pulling you toward IT managed services?
- What do you need from a sales manager to do your best work?
- What would make you leave this role in two years?

## Red Flags to Listen For

- Vague or defensive answer on Ahola — "it just wasn't a fit" without real explanation
- Comp expectations significantly above $75–80K base
- Can't describe a long-cycle consultative sale — only talks about volume/activity
- "I'm not technical — I'd rely on the team for that" (Phil's failure mode — probe depth)
- Can't name any specific Columbus contacts or warm relationships for IT buyers
- No research on Revolution Group — shows low investment in the process
- Frames success purely in terms of activity (calls/week) rather than outcomes (revenue closed)

## Green Lights

- Clean explanation of Ahola that makes sense
- Comp expectations in range
- Can articulate the SMB IT buyer's pain in business terms without being coached
- Mentions specific Columbus contacts or industries where she has depth
- Talks about moving from activity to pipeline to closed revenue — not just dials
