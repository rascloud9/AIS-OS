## Security (S1)

**S1.1** — Security policies are established and periodically reviewed and approved. Potential internal control deficiencies are evaluated and mitigated.

**S1.2** — All internal Revolution Group data and customer files will be properly classified and protected. Access rights and restrictions will be documented, and customer data storage and destruction requirements will be followed.

**S1.3** — Security risks are assessed on a periodic basis.

**S1.4** — Unauthorized physical access to Revolution Group premises will be prevented.

**S1.5** — Procedures are followed for all users requiring access to the Revolution Group internal and/or production networks.

**S1.6** — Accountability and responsibility for Physical and Logical security is assigned and maintained, and physical and logical system changes and maintenance is also assigned and maintained.

**S1.7** — All system components are tested and evaluated before implementation.

**S1.8** — Complaints and requests related to security issues are resolved in an efficient manner.

**S1.9** — All security breaches and incidents are identified and mitigated quickly and efficiently.

**S1.10** — Training and other resources are available to support system security policies.

**S1.11** — All situations that are not specifically addressed by Revolution Group policies will be handled in a proper manner.

**S1.12** — Responsibility for developing and maintaining system security policies and changes is assigned to a specific group.

**S1.13** — Procedures have been implemented to provide that confidential information is disclosed to parties only in accordance with Revolution Group's defined confidentiality and related security policies. The transmission, movement, and removal of information is restricted to authorized internal and external users and is protected. Confidential information within the boundaries of the system is protected against unauthorized access, use, and disclosure during input, processing, retention, output, and disposition.

**S1.14** — Confidentiality and related security policies, including any changes or updates, are communicated to internal and external users, vendors, and other third parties whose products and services are part of the system. In the event that a disclosed confidentiality practice is discontinued or changed to be less restrictive, procedures have been implemented to protect confidential information and to communicate these changes to external parties.

**S1.15** — Procedures have been implemented to obtain assurance or representation that the policies of vendors and other third parties to whom information is transferred are in conformity with Revolution Group policies related to confidentiality. Revolution Group assesses, periodically and as-needed, vendor and third party compliance with confidentiality commitments and system requirements.

**S1.16** — Change management processes are initiated when deficiencies in the design or operating effectiveness of controls are identified during system operation and monitoring.

**S1.17** — Accountability and responsibility for communicating significant security events to external parties is assigned and maintained. Procedures have been implemented to guide how those significant security events are communicated.

**S1.18** — The proper disposal of confidential client information is documented and followed. Inappropriate disposal of Revolution Group's confidential client information may put employees and Revolution Group at risk.

**S1.19** — The proper disposal/sanitization/destruction of media (physical or electronic) is documented and followed. Inappropriate disposal of customer data or media may put employees and Revolution Group at risk.

## Availability (A1)

**A1.1** — Revolution Group operations will consistently function in accordance with documented customer commitments as shown in signed agreements.

**A1.2** — Revolution Group monitors and evaluates system capacity to achieve customer commitments and other agreements regarding system availability.

---

## Confidentiality (C1)

**C1.1** — IT records and documents are adequately protected and maintained. Records no longer needed or of no value are discarded at the proper time. This covers both confidential information and backup information, including electronic documents (email, web files, text files, PDFs, Office files).

**C1.2** — The proper disposal/sanitization/destruction of media (physical or electronic) is documented and followed. Disposal aligns with contractual obligations and applicable laws. Confidential information no longer required is regularly and systematically destroyed, erased, or made anonymous. Disposal is documented.
