**Purpose:** Screen inbound leads for fit before routing to sales

## Qualification Logic

### Hard Disqualifiers
| Answer | Action |
|---|---|
| Under 10 employees | Disqualify — do not route to sales |
| Outside Central Ohio (fully managed inquiry) | Deprioritize — flag as out-of-territory |

### Service Routing
| Employee Count | IT Situation (Q3) | Route As |
|---|---|---|
| 10–250 | No dedicated IT, or using another provider | **Fully managed prospect** |
| 10–250 | Has internal IT staff | **Co-managed prospect** |
| 200–250 | Has internal IT staff | **Co-managed prospect** |
| 250+ | Any | Flag for review — co-managed only, needs sales conversation |
| "It's a mix" | Any | Needs a discovery call to sort out |

### Lead Priority Signals
These answers should elevate a lead's priority regardless of size:

- **Q4 — "We stop completely"**: strong buying urgency — route to sales fast
- **Q6 — Compliance requirement (Yes)**: higher urgency and budget; flag for compliance-aware sales pitch
- **Q7 — "ASAP" or "1–3 months"**: active buying window — prioritize follow-up

---

## Notes for Marketing Team

- Keep the form to 8 questions max — any longer and completion rates drop
- Q1 and Q2 are the primary filters; consider using conditional logic to end the form early for disqualified leads (under 10 employees or outside service area) rather than showing a generic rejection
- Q3 drives service routing — "fully managed" and "co-managed" prospects need different nurture sequences and sales messaging
- Q4 is a pain amplifier — it surfaces urgency and helps sales prioritize callbacks
- Q5–Q6 add context for the first sales conversation; not hard filters but useful for personalization
